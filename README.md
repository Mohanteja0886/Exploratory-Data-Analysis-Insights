World Happiness Report 2019 Analysis
⌒ Overview
This project provides an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) of the 2019 World Happiness Report. It explores the various factors—such as economic production, social support, and life expectancy—that contribute to global happiness levels across 156 countries.

ဒ Key Insights
Wealth & Happiness: A strong positive correlation (0.79) exists between GDP per capita and Happiness Scores.
Social Support: Community and government safety nets are the second most influential factor in high-ranking nations.
Nordic Dominance: Finland, Denmark, and Norway consistently lead the rankings due to balanced performance across all metrics.
The Generosity Paradox: Surprisingly, Generosity has a relatively low correlation with the overall happiness score compared to structural factors like Health and GDP.
ဒ Dataset Variables
Overall rank: Country's happiness rank.
Score: A metric measured by asking sampled people the question: "How would you rate your happiness on a scale of 0 to 10?"
GDP per capita: The extent to which GDP contributes to the calculation of the Happiness Score.
Social support: The extent to which Social support contributes.
Healthy life expectancy: The extent to which Life expectancy contributes.
Freedom to make life choices: The extent to which Freedom contributes.
Generosity: The extent to which Generosity contributes.
Perceptions of corruption: The extent to which Perception of Corruption contributes.
ဒ Visualizations Included
Top 10 Happiest Countries: Bar chart showing Finland as the leader.
Global Distribution: Histogram of happiness scores centered around an average of 5.41.
GDP vs. Happiness: Scatter plot illustrating the strong linear relationship.
Correlation Heatmap: A matrix showing how all factors (GDP, Social Support, etc.) interact with the total Score.
ဒ Methodology
Data Cleaning: Handled duplicates and verified no missing values exist in the final dataset.
EDA: Answered 5 specific research questions regarding global rankings and averages.
Libraries Used: Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn.
